Objective: To gain insight into how consumers use Fitbit smart devices

  • Segmenting Fitbit's users by activity level, correlating their behavior with key metrics like calories burned, and identifying weekly patterns

Analysis & Key Steps

  • Data Aggregation & Cleaning: Consolidated daily activity data from 30 unique users using R. Data was cleaned to address inconsistent entries, filter out extreme outliers, and prepare metrics for statistical analysis.
  • User Segmentation: Segmented users into groups (e.g., "Sedentary," "Lightly Active," "Very Active") based on their daily step count (e.g., <5k, 5k-10k, >10k steps) to identify distinct behavioral patterns.
  • Time-Based Trend Analysis: Analyzed usage patterns by day of the week to determine peak activity days when users are most likely to drop off.
  • Visualization & Insights: Created an interactive dashboard in Tableau to visually compare active vs. sedentary minutes.

Key Finding & Recommendation

  • Finding: A significant portion of users (the "Lightly Active" group) wear their device consistently but fail to meet the 7,000 steps per day threshold recommended for health benefits. Usage drops significantly on weekends compared to weekdays.
  • Recommendation: Develop targeted in-app motivational messages and challenges specifically for Lightly Active users on weekends. Recommendations should focus on short, achievable goals like the "Weekend Warrior 20-Minute Walk" to encourage continuous engagement and transition them into the "Very Active" category.
